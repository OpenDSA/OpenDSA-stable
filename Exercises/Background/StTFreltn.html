A relation is reflexive if a holds the relationship with itself for all values a in the set.

A relation is symmetric if everytime that a is related to b, then b is also related to a.

A relation is antisymmetric if everytime that a is related to b, then b is not related to a.

A relation is transitive if everytime that a is related to b and also b is related to c, then a is related to c.
